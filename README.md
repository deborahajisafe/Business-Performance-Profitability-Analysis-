# Business-Performance-Profitability-Analysis-
Analysis of sales performance, profitability drivers, customer behavior, and the impact of discounting on business outcomes 

## Project Overview

This project analyzes four years of retail sales data (2014–2017) for a fictional retail company, Superstore Giant. The objective was to evaluate sales performance, identify profitability drivers, and uncover operational issues affecting business growth.

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel, SQL Server, and Power BI.

## Business Problem

Although revenue continued to grow over the four-year period, management needed to understand why profitability was not improving at the same rate. The goal was to identify factors impacting margins and provide recommendations to improve overall business performance.

## Tools Used

* Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning & Validation)
* SQL Server (Data Exploration & Analysis)
* Power BI (Dashboard Development)

## Dataset

* Superstore Sales Dataset
* 9,994 transactions
* Period: 2014–2017
* Multiple dimensions including:

  * Sales
  * Profit
  * Discounts
  * Product Categories
  * Customer Segments
  * Regions
  * Shipping Modes

## Key Business Questions

1. How has revenue and profit changed over time?
2. Which products and categories drive profitability?
3. Which regions and states are underperforming?
4. How do discounts affect profit margins?
5. Which customer segments generate the highest returns?
6. What operational opportunities exist for improvement?

## Key Findings

### Revenue Growth vs Profitability

* Revenue increased by 51% between 2014 and 2017.
* Order volume nearly doubled.
* Profit margins failed to improve proportionally, indicating inefficiencies in growth.

### Discounting Is Destroying Profit

* 18.7% of all orders were loss-making.
* Orders discounted above 50% generated a total loss of $76,559.
* Removing high-discount orders could improve profit by approximately 26.7%.

### Furniture Category Underperformance

* Furniture generated over $742K in revenue.
* Profit margin was only 2.49%.
* Tables and Bookcases consistently generated losses.

### Regional Performance Issues

* Central Region recorded the lowest profitability.
* Texas and Illinois were among the largest loss-making states.
* Excessive discounting was strongly associated with regional losses.

### Customer Segment Insights

* Home Office customers delivered the highest profit margin.
* Office Supplies sold to Home Office customers produced the strongest segment-category performance.

## Business Recommendations

1. Implement a company-wide discount cap of 20%.
2. Restructure pricing strategy for Furniture products.
3. Conduct a profitability review within the Central region.
4. Increase investment in Home Office customers.
5. Focus growth efforts on high-performing states while recovering underperforming markets.

## Project Outcome

The analysis revealed that Superstore Giant's primary challenge is not revenue generation but discount management. Addressing excessive discounting alone could significantly improve profitability without requiring additional sales growth.
